Agricultural monitoring is the front line of defense in protecting the primary pathway to human physiology. Radionuclides are ingested through the food chain; therefore, managing the "rain-out" collection on crops is essential to prevent internal exposure and chronic systemic degradation.

- **Priority Monitoring:** Green leafy vegetables (chard, lettuce, spinach) and complex surfaces (broccoli, cauliflower) that trap radioactive particles.
- **High-Risk Selection:** Strawberries, which lack protective skins, facilitate the direct absorption of isotopic particulates.
- **Interim Strategy:** All open-air produce in affected latitudes must be avoided for a minimum of one year unless grown in controlled environments.

#### Dairy Supply Chain Moratorium

The dairy supply chain represents an extreme bioaccumulation risk. I recommend a **one-year moratorium** on all Northern Hemisphere dairy consumption. #### Tactical Decontamination for Root Vegetables

Root vegetables are shielded by soil but require strict processing commands to remove surface-borne radionuclides:

1. **Scrubbing:** Vigorously clean the exterior to remove soil-laden particulates.
2. **Peeling:** Remove all outer skins where radioactive particles adhere.
3. **Boiling:** Submerge vegetables in water and bring to a sustained boil for several minutes.
4. **Discarding:** Immediately discard the initial boiling water, as it contains the extracted radionuclides, and finish cooking in fresh, purified water.

The security of the food supply is fundamentally dependent on the absolute purity of water resources.
