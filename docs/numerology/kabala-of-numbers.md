# Kabala of Numbers I & II (Sepharial) {#title}

[[toc]]

Based on _The Kabala of Numbers_ (Parts I and II) by Sepharial, this text adds a **numerological and vibrational syntax** to the theory of the human biocomputer. While Lilly and Wilson focus on the _software_ of beliefs and the _circuits_ of evolution, Sepharial provides the **mathematical code** and the **cosmic timing mechanisms** that govern how that software interacts with the universal "mainframe."

## Part One {#part-one}

This text explores **numerology and kabalism** not merely as symbolic traditions, but as a systematic study of the **occult connections** between mathematical quantities and universal laws. The author posits that the universe is a **modification of cosmic forces** where numbers act as seals of natural law, reflecting the same **geometrical and archetypal relations** found in astronomy and physics. By analyzing the nine digits and their planetary correspondences, the source argues that **individual character and destiny** are determined by these numerical vibrations.

The structure of the work moves from theoretical foundations to practical systems of **divination and calculation**. It details various methods, such as the **Pythagorean, Hebraic, and Tarotic** schemes, to decode the hidden significance of names, birth dates, and historical cycles. A central theme is the **law of periodicity**, suggesting that human events recur in predictable waves—specifically the **nine-year cycle**—governed by the same mechanical intelligence that directs the planets.

Ultimately, the text serves as a **philosophical defense of optimism**, framing the world as a "Grand Man" or macrocosm where every soul is evolving through a **cyclic progression**. By mastering the "universal language" of numbers, the author suggests that individuals can gain **intellectual discernment** over their lives and align themselves with the **divine geometry** of the cosmos. The purpose of the work is to transition these ancient sciences into **practical tools** for understanding the "Why" of existence through the lens of quantitative values.

## Part Two {#part-two}

This handbook by Sepharial serves as an advanced guide to **Numerology**, expanding on earlier principles to explore the **quantitive relations** between sound, number, form, and color. The text posits that the universe is governed by a **geometric law of mind**, where numbers act as the primary key to understanding human **character and destiny**. By examining various systems of evaluation, such as the **Universal or Phonetic system**, the author demonstrates how individual lives resonate with specific **planetary vibrations** and periodic cycles. Ultimately, the work seeks to bridge the gap between **science and superstition**, arguing that the study of numerical ratios allows individuals to achieve **adaptability and symmetry** within their greater cosmic environment.
